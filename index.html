MASc. Thesis - Blood Vessel Growth Simulator (2017)

Developed an internal simulation software to help Bioscience Researchers design experimental microfluidic devices (top part of the figure) and determine the necessary concentration of chemicals (denoted v' on the bottom graph) to induce cell (denoted ρ') movement representative of blood vessel growth. My thesis consisted of:

  • Translating images into model verification data
  • Building and implementing mathematical models that can identify the optimal device configuration
  • Deploying a simple user interface for researchers.

The outcome of this project was a reduction in time spent planning experiments by 40% and improvement in experiment success by 200%, which translated into savings of ~$20k/year for the lab.

Technologies: Matlab, Python, Docker
